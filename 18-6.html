Why It Matters

Stress loads the system not only through effort, but through stillness without release.
When movement is steady and non-demanding, it helps the body exit survival mode without collapsing energy.
In daily life, this often shows up as smoother evenings, fewer spikes of irritability, and better ability to wind down.

What's Really Happening

The key mechanism today involves venous return ‚Äî how blood flows back to the heart.
Gentle, rhythmic movement activates the muscles of the legs and torso, which lightly squeeze veins and push blood upward.
This improves circulation without raising heart rate aggressively.
As blood returns more efficiently, pressure inside the heart normalizes and the heart doesn't need to compensate with force.
The nervous system reads this as resolution rather than demand.
In daily life, this often feels like warmth in the limbs, quieter thoughts, and a sense of release rather than fatigue.
