Why Touch Calms the Nervous System

From birth, your body learns to associate steady, warm contact with safety.
Touch-sensitive nerves in the skin ‚Äî called C-tactile fibers ‚Äî connect directly to brain regions that regulate heart rate, emotion, and social bonding.

That's why a hug, a hand on your heart, or even self-touch can slow breathing and quiet your mind.
You don't have to talk yourself into calm ‚Äî your skin already knows the language.
