Why It Matters

Your immune system depends on reserve. When you're well-rested, nourished, and recovering between demands, it responds smoothly and quietly.
When recovery time shrinks, immunity doesn't shut down ‚Äî it becomes less efficient.
This can show up as low energy, frequent "almost getting sick," or feeling run down without a clear reason.
Understanding this helps you respond with support instead of pushing through.

What's Really Happening

One key process involved here is immune reserve availability.
Your body constantly produces and uses immune cells to handle everyday challenges ‚Äî stress, microbes, tissue repair.
During periods of rest, sleep, and low stress, these reserves are replenished. When stress is constant or sleep is irregular, replenishment slows.
The immune system still functions, but with less margin. This doesn't mean illness is inevitable ‚Äî it means recovery takes longer.
That's when your body starts sending subtle signals like fatigue or feeling "off."
