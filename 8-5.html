Why It Matters

Hair follicles are highly sensitive to internal signals. They constantly decide: grow or pause.
Under chronic stress:
  • cortisol stays elevated,
  • blood flow is redirected away from "non-essential" tissues,
  • inflammation increases,
  • growth signals are suppressed.
The body chooses survival over appearance.
Hair doesn't fall out because it's weak. It falls out because the system is under pressure.

How Stress Disrupts Hair Cycles

Hair grows in cycles:
Anagen
‚Äî growth,
Catagen
‚Äî transition,
Telogen
‚Äî rest/shedding.
Chronic stress pushes more follicles prematurely into the rest/shedding phase (telogen effluvium).
This often shows up as:
  • diffuse hair shedding,
  • thinning ponytail,
  • more hair on the brush or shower drain,
  • hair that stops growing longer.
Importantly: hair loss often appears 2‚Äì3 months after the stress peak ‚Äî which makes the cause hard to recognize.
