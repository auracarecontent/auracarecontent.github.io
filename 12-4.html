What is Oxidative Stress?

Oxidative stress is basically "cellular wear and tear" that builds up when your body is doing a lot ‚Äî and doesn't get enough recovery time.
Every time your body produces energy (especially when you're stressed, underslept, or constantly "on"), it creates tiny by-products. That's normal.
One simple way to describe these by-products is free radicals ‚Äî highly reactive molecules your body naturally makes during metabolism and also in response to things like stress, pollution, or intense training.
Your body also has its own cleanup and protection system ‚Äî often called antioxidant defenses ‚Äî that neutralizes and clears these molecules.
But if free radicals build up faster than your body can balance them, your cells experience more strain over time. That's what we call oxidative stress.
Think of it like:
  • üì± leaving your phone running with too many apps open
  • üè† or never giving your room time for a quick daily tidy
Small "cleanup windows" ‚Äî especially at night ‚Äî help your body keep oxidative stress from piling up.
