DAY 1

Do you actually need fasting

🧠

Daily focus

Many people hear that fasting boosts metabolism and energy. But some feel better with it, while others feel worse.
This does not mean something is wrong. Bodies respond to food timing in different ways.
☀️

Fasting is not for everyone

Fasting is often presented as a universal solution.
But the body's needs depend on sleep, stress, activity, and how stable your energy already is.
Some people naturally go several hours between meals and feel calm and clear. Others feel shaky, anxious, or mentally foggy if they wait too long.
Both responses are normal.
The goal is not to force fasting. The goal is to notice how your body reacts to breaks between meals.
For some people, gentle food pauses help energy feel steadier. For others, regular meals feel safer and more supportive.
Understanding your own response is the first step.
😌

🧠

The hunger signal your stomach sends

One key signal in this process is a hormone called ghrelin.
Ghrelin is produced in the stomach. Its job is simple: tell the brain when it may be time to eat.
When you follow a regular eating rhythm, ghrelin begins to follow a schedule. It rises before meals and drops after eating.
But when meals are irregular, the signal becomes less predictable. Hunger can appear suddenly or disappear completely.
This is why some people feel calm during longer meal gaps, while others feel tense or shaky.
Understanding this signal helps explain why fasting supports some bodies — and not others.
What ghrelin does
📈
It rises before expected meals — signaling the brain to prepare for food.
📉
It drops after eating — helping hunger feel calmer and more manageable.
