What's Really Happening

A key organ involved in everyday detox is the liver.
The liver constantly filters blood and breaks down used hormones, medications, and metabolic byproducts.
Part of this waste is packaged into bile, a fluid the liver produces.
Bile moves into the digestive tract and carries this waste out of the body through regular elimination.
When meals are regular and stress is lower, bile production and movement stay steady.
When stress is high or meals are skipped, bile movement can slow.
