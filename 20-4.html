DAY 4

The quiet fix for afternoon energy crashes

🧠

Daily focus

The afternoon often brings a familiar shift.
Energy fades. Focus becomes harder. The body starts looking for coffee, chocolate, or something sweet.
The morning may have felt clear and productive. Then suddenly the body feels slower and less steady.
⚡

Why afternoon hunger appears

When meals digest very quickly, the body stops receiving "we're satisfied" signals sooner. The brain notices that fuel from the last meal is fading — appetite slowly turns back on.
That is why the body starts asking for snacks, sugar, or caffeine.
Fiber helps these signals last longer.
Meals that include fiber help satiety signals last longer. Energy and appetite stay more stable through the afternoon.

The gut signals that control appetite

The intestine releases special satiety hormones after food arrives — simple chemical signals that tell the brain: "food is here."
🔵
PYY — Peptide YY
Reduces appetite and slows digestion slightly. Tells the brain: slow down, we have enough.
🟢
GLP-1
Helps the body feel satisfied and keeps food moving through digestion at a steady pace.
Fiber helps activate both signals.
When fiber reaches the intestine, these satiety hormones are released more strongly. The brain receives a clearer message that the body has eaten enough.
💡

Plan to action — Support your satiety signals

🥦 Step 1 — Add one fiber-rich food to lunch today
Beans, lentils, vegetables, oats, or seeds — any of these activate satiety hormones in the intestine.
🥚 Step 2 — Include some protein in the same meal
Protein works alongside fiber to help satiety signals last even longer.
⏱️ Step 3 — Notice how your appetite feels later in the afternoon
Pay attention to whether the urge for coffee or snacks arrives later or feels less strong.
🧠 Why this works
Fiber triggers the release of PYY and GLP-1 in the intestine — hormones that tell the brain to stay satisfied.

When these signals stay active longer, appetite stays quieter and afternoon energy stays steadier.
