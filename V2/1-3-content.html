Why your brain feels low on battery

Your brain makes up only ~2% of body weight, but uses ~20% of your daily energy.
When fuel delivery is uneven, thinking feels heavy and slow — even simple tasks require effort. Clarity comes from steady energy, oxygen, hydration, and reduced stress signals.
⚡
When glucose rises too fast (spike):
Energy feels jittery, unfocused, overstimulated.
📉
When glucose drops too low (crash):
The brain lacks fuel — thinking slows, fog and heaviness appear.
When glucose stays within a stable range, neurons fire smoothly — and focus feels clear and steady.
🧠

4 easy steps to clear brain fog

Step 1 — Morning: Build a Stable Fuel Base
Balanced breakfast with protein + fiber keeps glucose steady and supports neurotransmitters (with help from B-vitamins and folate) — this sets up calm, clear focus for the first half of the day.
Step 2 — Mid-Morning: Add a 2-Minute Oxygen Reset
Standing up or stretching for 1–2 minutes boosts blood flow and oxygen delivery to the brain, helping you shake off early brain fog and stay alert.
Step 3 — Afternoon: Hydrate Your Focus
A simple water or herbal tea break restores hydration, supports smooth blood flow, and prevents the low-grade fog that often shows up as irritability or mental heaviness.
Step 4 — Evening: Protect Tomorrow's Clarity
Dim lights and a gentle wind-down routine reduce late-day overstimulation, helping your nervous system switch into recovery mode — so you wake up with a clearer brain.
Spencer et al., 2020, Nutrition Reviews; Benton & Donahue, 2021, Front Nutr; Petersen et al., 2020, J Appl Physiol.
