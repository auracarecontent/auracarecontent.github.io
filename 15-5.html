Why It Matters

Low-level daily exposure increases background load on the body.
This keeps the nervous system slightly alert, even when nothing feels "wrong."
Reducing exposure lowers the amount your body has to process each day.
This often brings subtle relief in energy, focus, or digestion.

What's Really Happening

The key organ involved here is the liver.
The liver processes substances that enter the body through food, skin, and air.
The more input it receives, the more work it has to do.
When exposure drops, liver workload decreases.
This frees energy for other functions like digestion and repair.
The result is often a quieter, steadier internal state.
Less input means less internal effort.
