Build Your Personal Sleep Plan (4 Simple Decisions)

This isn't a routine. It's a set of protective choices you make during the day.
Step 1 ‚Äî Choose One Energy Boundary
Decide one clear limit that protects your evening energy:
stop work at a fixed time,
avoid heavy conversations after dinner,
no caffeine after a certain hour.
This boundary tells your system the day won't drag into the night.
Step 2 ‚Äî Simplify the Evening
Remove one decision from your evening:
a simple, repeatable dinner,
the same low-effort evening order,
fewer options, fewer choices.
Less deciding = less nervous system load.
Step 3 ‚Äî Set a Gentle Night Expectation
Decide this before night:
"Even if sleep isn't perfect, I allow my body to rest."
This reduces pressure before it has a chance to build.
Step 4 ‚Äî Protect the Morning After
Choose one supportive rule for tomorrow:
wake up around the same time,
don't punish yourself for a bad night,
keep the morning light and calm.
Sleep improves when the morning doesn't threaten the night.
