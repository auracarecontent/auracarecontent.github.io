The Science Behind the Signals

Your resting heart rate is the number of beats per minute when you're calm and still ‚Äî a direct reflection of how efficiently your heart works.
  • A healthy adult's RHR usually sits between 60‚Äì80 bpm.
  • Lower values often mean stronger cardiovascular fitness and better vagal tone.
Your heart-rate variability (HRV) is the tiny difference in time between one heartbeat and the next.
It shows how flexible your nervous system is ‚Äî how well it can switch between alertness and recovery.
üëâ Higher HRV = better adaptability, lower stress load, and a more resilient heart.
üí° When stress dominates, the body tightens and HRV drops.
When recovery wins, your heart finds rhythm again ‚Äî smooth, steady, and calm.

Why HRV and RHR Matter Together

Think of them as partners:
  • RHR shows how hard your heart is working on average.
  • HRV shows how well your nervous system adjusts to change.
When both improve, it means your vagus nerve is strong, your blood vessels are flexible, and your heart recovers quickly after stress.
But when RHR stays high and HRV stays low, it's an early signal of overload ‚Äî often days before you feel tired or anxious.
Your heart always knows before your mind catches up.
