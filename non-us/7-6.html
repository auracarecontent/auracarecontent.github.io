How Caffeine Really Affects Head Pain

Caffeine works through two main pathways:
üß† Brain alertness
Caffeine blocks adenosine ‚Äî the molecule that signals tiredness.
This temporarily increases alertness and reduces pain perception.
ü©∏ Blood vessel tone
Caffeine causes vasoconstriction (narrowing of blood vessels).
That's why it can reduce headache pain short-term.
But here's the catch:
With regular use, the brain adapts.
Blood vessels become more sensitive.
When caffeine drops (even slightly), vessels dilate ‚Äî triggering pain.
That's why:
  • caffeine can relieve headaches,
  • but withdrawal causes headaches,
  • and irregular intake increases migraine frequency.

What Actually Helps

Migraines worsen when caffeine intake is:
  • irregular,
  • used to "push through" fatigue,
  • combined with poor sleep or skipped meals.
Relief comes from predictability, not elimination.
