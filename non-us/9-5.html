How Rhythm Controls Hormones

Your body runs on a 24-hour internal clock. This clock uses light as its main signal:
  • morning light tells the body to wake up and produce cortisol,
  • darkness tells it to release melatonin,
  • melatonin allows repair and hormone production overnight.
When light comes at the wrong time:
  • bright screens late at night,
  • little daylight in the morning,
  • irregular sleep hours,
the clock gets confused. Cortisol and melatonin overlap when they shouldn't ‚Äî and sex hormones are caught in between.
Desire doesn't disappear. It loses its window.
