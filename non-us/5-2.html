Your gut is like a smart garden. Inside it live tiny helpers ‚Äî bacteria ‚Äî that turn healthy food into messages your body understands.

The Microbiome ‚Äì GLP-1 Link

Think of fiber as food for your good bacteria.
When these bacteria eat fiber, they create tiny natural messengers called short-chain fatty acids (SCFAs).
SCFAs travel to your gut cells and whisper, "Make more GLP-1!"
GLP-1 then talks to your brain through the vagus nerve, helping you feel calm and satisfied instead of craving more food.
The more variety of plants you eat, the happier and more balanced this conversation becomes ‚Äî your appetite softens, mood steadies, and energy lasts.
