The Science of Lipids and Pressure

Cholesterol itself isn't harmful ‚Äî your body needs it to build hormones and cell membranes.
But not all cholesterol behaves the same way:
LDL (low-density lipoprotein)
Often called "bad" cholesterol, but it's really your delivery system ‚Äî it carries cholesterol to your cells. When there's too much LDL or when it becomes oxidized by stress and inflammation, it starts irritating the vessel walls and forming plaque.
HDL (high-density lipoprotein)
The opposite ‚Äî your cleanup system. It collects excess cholesterol from the bloodstream and returns it to the liver for safe recycling.
When LDL climbs too high or HDL drops too low, vessels lose elasticity and blood pressure rises.
Meanwhile, triglycerides, another type of fat, increase with sugar spikes, alcohol, and chronic stress ‚Äî making blood thicker and harder to pump.

Why It Matters

Your nervous system directly influences lipid metabolism.
When vagal tone is strong, digestion and fat transport stay balanced; when stress dominates, cortisol and insulin drive triglycerides higher and HDL lower.
This means your calm habits ‚Äî not just your diet ‚Äî shape your long-term vessel health.
Movement, recovery sleep, and mindful eating protect your arteries as much as supplements or medications.
