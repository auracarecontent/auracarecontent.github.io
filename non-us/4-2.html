What About the Vagus?

The vagus nerve is the main messenger between your two brains ‚Äî it carries emotional updates from your gut straight to your mind.
When vagal tone is strong, the gut's signals stay clear and steady: muscles relax, digestion stays smooth, and emotional reactions calm faster.
But when stress tightens the body, vagal signaling weakens, and the gut begins to amplify tension, sending "something's wrong" messages even when you're safe.
Strengthening the vagus brings the conversation back into balance ‚Äî your belly softens, your thoughts settle, and both brains feel supported again.
