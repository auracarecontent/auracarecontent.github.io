The Science of Stress and the Heart

Every emotion you experience ‚Äî anger, frustration, anxiety ‚Äî travels through your body as an electrical and hormonal signal.
Blood pressure is the force of blood against your artery walls. It must stay strong enough to move oxygen but not so strong that it damages vessels.
  • Adrenaline speeds up the heartbeat and tightens vessels ‚Äî like turning up water pressure in a hose.
  • Cortisol keeps the body on alert for hours, slowing repair and raising average pressure.
  • The vagus nerve usually steps in to slow your pulse and widen vessels, but chronic tension keeps it "muted."
Over time, this pattern reduces heart-rate variability (HRV) ‚Äî your body's ability to adapt ‚Äî and makes even small stressors feel heavier.
üëâ HRV means the tiny, natural time differences between heartbeats ‚Äî the higher it is, the calmer and more resilient you are.
üí° In short: the faster your world feels, the harder your heart works ‚Äî and the higher your blood pressure climbs.
