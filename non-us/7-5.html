Why It Matters

Many people fear food when migraines or dizziness appear:
  • "Was it cheese?"
  • "Was it coffee?"
  • "Should I stop eating everything?"
But migraines rarely react to one food.
They react to patterns:
  • blood sugar swings,
  • dehydration,
  • inflammatory load,
  • skipped meals followed by overload.
Food can either steady the system ‚Äî or add another stressor to an already full bucket.
üò¥

How Food Influences Head Symptoms

Your brain is extremely sensitive to:
  • glucose stability (fuel),
  • vascular tone (blood flow),
  • inflammation,
  • electrolyte balance.
When meals are irregular or overly stimulating:
  • blood vessels overreact,
  • nerves become more excitable,
  • head pressure and pain threshold drops.
When food is steady and supportive:
  • circulation smooths,
  • tension decreases,
  • symptoms become less frequent and less intense.
