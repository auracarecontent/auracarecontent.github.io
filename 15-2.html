Why It Matters

Detox myths often teach the body that it's under threat rather than supported.
Restriction, skipping meals, or "flushing" plans can quietly raise stress signals in the nervous system.
When that happens, the body shifts into protection mode and slows digestion, elimination, and repair.
This can increase fatigue, irritability, bloating, and mental fog ‚Äî exactly the opposite of what people hope for.
Knowing this helps reduce guilt and breaks the cycle of trying harder when the body actually needs ease.

What's Really Happening

One key process involved here is gut movement, also called intestinal motility.
Your intestines move waste forward using gentle muscular contractions.
These movements depend on regular food intake, hydration, and a calm nervous system.
When you restrict food or fast aggressively, the gut receives fewer signals to move.
Stress hormones also reduce these contractions.
As a result, waste moves more slowly through the intestines.
This often feels like bloating, pressure, or heaviness ‚Äî not because toxins increased, but because movement decreased.
