Understanding Your Body Signals (Simple & Friendly)

üíì Heart Rate
Your heart rate naturally rises when you're stressed or anxious.

This doesn't mean something is wrong with your heart.

It means your nervous system is saying: "Be ready."

A constantly elevated resting heart rate can be a sign that your body is spending too much time in alert mode and not enough time in recovery.
üìâ HRV (Heart Rate Variability)
HRV describes how much variation there is between heartbeats.

Higher HRV ‚Üí your nervous system is flexible and able to recover
Lower HRV ‚Üí your system is stuck in "on" mode

In anxiety, HRV often drops ‚Äî not because you're unhealthy, but because your body hasn't felt safe enough to relax.

Think of HRV as a measure of resilience, not fitness or willpower.
ü©∫ Blood Pressure
Blood pressure can rise temporarily during stress or anxiety.

This is a normal survival response ‚Äî it helps deliver blood to muscles and the brain.

But when stress is frequent, blood pressure can stay elevated longer than needed.

That's not a personal failure.
It's a sign your body hasn't had enough clear recovery signals.
