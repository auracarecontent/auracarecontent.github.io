Why It Matters

Movement can calm inflammation. But only when it matches your current capacity.
If your system is already stressed, very intense exercise can add more strain. That keeps the body slightly inflamed instead of helping it recover.
üß†

What's Really Happening

A key player here is your muscle tissue.
When you move, muscles contract and release small signaling proteins called myokines. Myokines help reduce inflammatory signals in the body.
But very intense or prolonged exercise also creates micro-damage in muscle fibers. This triggers a repair response.
Repair is normal and healthy. But if stress is already high, repeated intense training keeps inflammatory signals elevated longer than needed.
This often feels like deep fatigue, heavy legs, and longer recovery time.
