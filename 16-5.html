Why It Matters

Your immune system responds best to predictability.
Regular inputs help immune cells know when to activate and when to recover.

What's Really Happening

One key process involved here is immune rhythm regulation.
Immune cells follow daily rhythms linked to sleep, meals, and movement.
When meals are skipped or sleep shifts late, immune signaling becomes less precise.
Light movement improves circulation, helping immune cells reach tissues efficiently.
Short pauses reduce stress hormones that interfere with immune coordination.
Together, these signals tell the immune system when to work and when to reset.
This is why simple habits can have outsized effects.
