DAY 5

How long should you fast

🧠

Daily focus

Many people ask the same question about fasting. How long should the break between meals actually be?
Some try very long fasting windows right away. Others eat every two hours because they fear getting hungry.
The truth is simpler. The right fasting window depends on how your body uses energy during the day.
☀️

Longer is not always better

Fasting is often presented as a competition. People talk about 16 hours, 18 hours, even longer.
But the body does not need extreme food gaps to benefit from metabolic pauses.
For many people, a simple 12–14 hour overnight break already gives the body time to finish digestion and shift into stored energy use.
What matters most is consistency. A predictable rhythm helps the body prepare for digestion and recovery.
The goal is not the longest fast. The goal is a fasting window that feels stable and sustainable.

Your body runs on an internal clock

A key system involved here is the circadian rhythm.
This internal clock is controlled by a small area in the brain called the hypothalamus. It coordinates sleep, hormones, digestion, and energy use.
During the day, digestion and metabolism are more active. At night, the body shifts toward repair and recovery.
When eating continues late into the night, this rhythm becomes disrupted. The body tries to digest food while it should be slowing down.
An overnight fasting window allows digestion to finish before the body moves into night recovery.
Many people notice better sleep and steadier morning energy when eating follows this rhythm.
A day in two phases
☀️
Daytime — digestion and metabolism are active, energy is produced and used.
🌙
Night — the body shifts to repair and recovery. Digestion should be finished.
💡

Plan to action — Try a simple overnight food break

1. Check your dinner time
Look at the time you usually finish dinner.
2. Plan breakfast 12 hours later
Tomorrow morning, eat breakfast about 12 hours after dinner. Example: if dinner ends at 7 PM, have breakfast around 7–8 AM.
3. Notice your morning hunger
In the morning, notice how your hunger feels. Is it calm, strong, or barely there?
🧠 Why this works
This small overnight break gives the body time to finish digestion before the next day begins. The body does not need a long fast — it needs a consistent one.

Rhythm matters more than duration.
✨ Key takeaway
Your body prefers rhythm over extremes. A sustainable window beats a perfect one.
