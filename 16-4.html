Why It Matters

Your immune system constantly builds, repairs, and replaces immune cells.
That work depends on nutrients coming in every day, not occasionally.
When food lacks variety or key nutrients, immune recovery slows.
This doesn't always show up as illness ‚Äî it often shows up as fatigue, frequent "almost sick" days, or low energy.
Understanding this shifts the focus from eating more to eating better.
üß†

What's Really Happening

One key process involved here is immune cell production.
Immune cells are short-lived and must be continuously replaced.
To do that, your body needs amino acids, minerals, and vitamins as raw materials.
If meals are repetitive, ultra-processed, or low in nutrient density, production slows.
The immune system still works, but with fewer fresh cells available. This reduces reserve and response speed.
That's why immunity can feel low even when meals are regular.
