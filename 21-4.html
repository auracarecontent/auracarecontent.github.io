DAY 4

5 foods that make fasting easier

🧠

Daily focus

Some people find fasting effortless. Others feel hungry within an hour of their last meal.
The difference often comes down to what that meal was made of.
☀️

The plate that keeps hunger quiet

Two meals can have the same calories and feel completely different.
A plate built mostly from fast carbohydrates is digested quickly. Energy rises fast and then drops.
But when meals contain protein and fiber, digestion moves differently.
Food leaves the stomach more slowly. Energy is released gradually. The body receives a stronger signal that it has been properly fed.
This is why balanced meals naturally reduce the urge to snack.
Fast carbohydrate plate
🍞 Fast carbs 80%🥩 Protein 10%🥦 Fiber 10%
Hunger returns in 1–2 hours
Balanced plate
🍞 Carbs 35%🥩 Protein 35%🥦 Fiber 30%
Fullness lasts 3–4 hours
🧠

The fullness signal from your gut

A key signal involved in fullness is a hormone called GLP-1.
GLP-1 is released by cells in the small intestine when food enters the gut. It sends a message to the brain that enough food has arrived.
Protein and fiber stimulate this hormone strongly. They also slow how quickly the stomach empties food into the intestine.
When this signal is strong, the brain receives a clear message that the body is satisfied. Hunger stays calm for longer.
This is why balanced plates often make fasting windows feel much easier.
⭐

5 foods that make fasting easier

These foods support fullness and steady energy between meals.
🥚
Eggs
Rich in protein that helps the body stay satisfied for longer.
🥛
Greek yogurt
Provides protein and healthy fats that slow digestion.
🫘
Beans and lentils
Contain both protein and fiber, which support longer-lasting fullness.
🥦
Vegetables
High in fiber that slows digestion and stabilizes hunger signals.
🌾
Oats or whole grains
Provide slow carbohydrates and fiber that release energy gradually.

Science facts

🧬
Protein and fiber strongly influence hormones that regulate hunger
Meals rich in these nutrients increase GLP-1 and other satiety signals. These signals slow stomach emptying and extend the feeling of fullness.
🍽️
Balanced meals containing protein and fiber reduce snacking and improve appetite regulation
Source: Drucker DJ — Cell Metabolism, 2018 · Leidy HJ — American Journal of Clinical Nutrition, 2015 · Slavin JL — Nutrition, 2013
