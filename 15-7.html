Why It Matters

Your body relies on food to keep waste moving out steadily, not to "flush" anything aggressively.
Fiber-rich and nutrient-dense foods lower the workload on the liver and gut.
They support regular elimination, stable energy, and mental clarity.
This approach calms the nervous system instead of activating control or fear.
Consistency matters far more than strict rules.

What's Really Happening

The key process here is fiber binding in the gut.
Fiber absorbs water and adds bulk to stool.
This helps waste move smoothly through the intestines.
Fiber also binds substances the liver sends into bile.
When bile-bound waste is carried out regularly, internal buildup is reduced.
This often feels like less bloating and more lightness.
No restriction is required ‚Äî just steady input.
