Why It Matters

What's Really Happening

One key player here is your immune system.
When it senses stress ‚Äî from lack of sleep, sugar spikes, or constant pressure ‚Äî it releases small signaling proteins called cytokines. These are chemical messengers.
Cytokines tell the body to stay alert and ready. That alert state uses fuel.
If cytokines stay slightly elevated for a long time, your body keeps burning energy in the background. Not enough to make you sick. But enough to make you feel drained.
This often shows up as heavy limbs, brain fog, and that "low battery" feeling ‚Äî even after rest.
