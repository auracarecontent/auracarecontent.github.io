Why It Matters

Stimulants don't create energy ‚Äî they borrow it.
They push your brain to work harder without improving how it's fueled or supported.
Over time, this widens the gap between effort and capacity.
That's when focus feels fragile and dependent on "one more boost."

What's Really Happening

The key process here is neurotransmitter depletion.
Your brain communicates using chemical messengers like dopamine and norepinephrine.
Caffeine and stimulants force the release of these messengers.
This temporarily increases alertness and drive.
But those messengers need time, nutrients, and rest to be replenished.
When release outpaces recovery, signaling becomes weaker and noisier.
That's when focus drops, motivation fades, and mental fatigue sets in.
