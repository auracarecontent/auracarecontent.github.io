Why It Matters

Low, steady irritation inside the body can build up from daily habits. Not from one big mistake.
When this builds slowly, it can affect mood, sleep depth, digestion, and how steady your energy feels. You may feel wired at night and flat during the day.

What's Really Happening

One simple trigger is blood sugar swings.
When you eat a meal high in refined carbs or sugar, your blood glucose rises quickly. Your pancreas then releases insulin. Insulin moves glucose from the blood into cells.
If this spike happens often, glucose can stay slightly elevated longer than it should. High glucose can irritate blood vessel walls.
In response, the body releases small inflammatory signals. Not dramatic. Just steady.
Over time, these repeated spikes can make you feel tired after meals, mentally foggy, and more reactive to stress.
