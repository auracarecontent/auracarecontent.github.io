DAY 6

When fasting can backfire

🧠

Daily focus

Fasting is often presented as a universal solution. But the body does not always respond the same way.
For some people fasting brings clarity and stable energy. For others it creates tension, fatigue, or strong cravings.
This does not mean fasting is bad. It means the body may be under too much pressure already.
☀️

When the body is already overloaded

Fasting adds a small stress signal to the body.
In a balanced system, this signal can be useful. The body adapts and becomes more efficient.
But if sleep is poor, stress is high, or meals are already too small, fasting can push the system further.
Instead of feeling steady, people may feel shaky, irritable, or mentally foggy.
The body is not failing. It is simply protecting its energy.
Understanding these signals helps you know when fasting supports your body — and when it asks for more support instead.

The stress hormone that keeps you going

A key hormone involved here is cortisol.
Cortisol is produced by the adrenal glands, small organs located above the kidneys. Its job is to help the body maintain energy during stress or food gaps.
When blood sugar drops, cortisol signals the liver to release stored glucose. This helps keep the brain supplied with energy.
But if the body is already under pressure from poor sleep or chronic stress, cortisol may rise more strongly.
This can create feelings of tension, anxiety, or sudden hunger.
In these moments, the body is not weak. It is asking for fuel and recovery.

Reflection prompt

How does your body feel when you wait longer between meals?
Do you feel calm and steady — or tense and drained?
✅

Plan to action — Adjust your fasting window

1. Think about your usual meal gaps
Think about the longest break between meals you usually have.
2. Adjust to what feels comfortable
Tomorrow adjust it slightly so it feels more comfortable for your body: if the gap feels too long — start your first meal a bit earlier. If the gap already feels easy — keep it the same.
3. Pay attention to how you feel
Notice energy, focus, and mood during the day. These signals are useful information.
🧠 Why this works
The goal is not the longest fasting window. The goal is the one where your body feels steady and supported.

A fasting window that fits your life works better than one that fights it.
✨ Key takeaway
Fasting should support your body, not challenge it.
