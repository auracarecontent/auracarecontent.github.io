Why It Matters

Sleep is when the body cools internal irritation.
If sleep is short, broken, or light, the system stays slightly alert. That alert state keeps inflammatory signals higher than they should be.
Over time, this affects mood, energy, patience, and how fast you recover from stress.
üß†

What's Really Happening

A key hormone involved here is cortisol.
Cortisol is released by your adrenal glands. It helps you wake up and respond to stress.
At night, cortisol should drop. This allows the immune system to repair tissues and calm inflammation.
When sleep is short or interrupted, cortisol stays elevated longer. This keeps the immune system in a more activated state.
The result feels like morning stiffness, low energy, and that wired-but-tired feeling during the day.
