Why It Matters

When stress stays on all day, the body stays in defense mode.
Defense mode uses energy. It also keeps inflammatory signals slightly elevated.
Over time, this affects digestion, sleep depth, and how fast you recover from small setbacks.

What's Really Happening

The key system here is your HPA axis.
This is the communication line between your brain and your adrenal glands.
When your brain senses pressure, it signals the adrenals to release cortisol. Cortisol increases heart rate, blood sugar, and alertness.
In short bursts, this is helpful. But when cortisol stays elevated for long periods, immune cells become more reactive.
They release more inflammatory messengers. This often feels like tension, shallow breathing, and that wired-but-exhausted state.
