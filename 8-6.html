Why It Matters

Many people drink enough water ‚Äî and still notice:
  • tight or dull skin,
  • fine lines that appear quickly,

That's because water alone doesn't hydrate tissue. Minerals move water into cells.
Without electrolytes:
  • water stays outside cells,
  • collagen fibers dry out,
  • skin loses resilience,
  • repair slows.
Hydration that heals is cellular, not just volumetric.

Why Skin Dehydrates Internally

Internal dehydration often happens when:
  • sodium, potassium, or magnesium are low,
  • stress increases mineral loss,
  • caffeine or sweating outpaces replenishment,
  • digestion or absorption is suboptimal.
In this state:
  • collagen fibers stiffen,
  • skin barrier weakens,
  • elasticity drops.
The skin doesn't lack water ‚Äî it lacks the ability to hold it.
